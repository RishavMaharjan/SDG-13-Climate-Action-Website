Reflection on the Validation Process

Using the W3C Markup Validation Service was an essential step in refining the interactive elements of my assigned pages. During initial testing, I discovered a missing closing </label> tag on the feedback form, which could have led to accessibility issues. Correcting this was a valuable reminder of how small structural errors can impact screen reader functionality.

On the Team Page, I initially used duplicate IDs for some of the hidden detail panels. The validator helped me identify these conflicts, which I resolved by switching to class based targeting for the hover effects. This process not only fixed the validation errors but also made my CSS more robust.

The Content Page development taught me the importance of landmarks and heading hierarchy. The validator's warnings about empty sections helped me maintain a cleaner document structure. Overall, the process confirmed that valid code is the foundation of an accessible and professional user experience, ensuring that our Climate Action website performs reliably across all devices.
