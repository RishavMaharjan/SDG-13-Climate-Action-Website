Sustainable Development Goal 13

Act now for our climate

Climate change is the defining challenge of our generation. Together as students, citizens, and future professionals, we have the power to make a real difference. This website is your guide to action.

Why Climate Action Matters

The Threat

The Crisis is Real

Climate change is no longer a future threat. Rising seas, extreme weather, and collapsing ecosystems are happening right now. Students and young people will inherit consequences of today's decisions.

Fig 4. Community allotments and urban growing projects reduce food miles, promote biodiversity, and strengthen local connections, all key aspects of grassroots climate resilience.

  • Extreme weather events have doubled since 2000
  • Sea levels rising 3.7mm per year and accelerating
  • 1 million species at risk of extinction
Action

Students Can Drive Change

You don't need to be a politician or scientist to make a meaningful impact. As a student, you have access to communities, knowledge, and platforms that can amplify climate action in powerful ways.

  • Switch to a plant-rich diet 3+ days a week
  • Join or start a sustainability group on campus
  • Use public transport or cycle instead of driving
Solutions

A Better Future is Possible

If individuals, communities and nations act together, we can limit warming to 1.5°C and build a fairer, cleaner world. Every action, no matter how small, contributes to this shared outcome.

  • Clean energy could power 90% of the world by 2050
  • Green jobs could reach 24 million globally by 2030
  • Restoring forests could absorb 10% of annual emissions
